Frequently Asked Questions

You May Ask

What are the reasons that may lead to privatisation of government-owned firms?
Privatisation helps governments save money and increase efficiency by allowing private companies to manage services and goods more effectively. It aims to reduce the financial burden on the government and improve service delivery.
What are some examples of privatisation in Kenya?
Recent examples of privatisation in Kenya include entities such as the Kenya Literature Bureau (KLB), Kenyatta International Convention Centre (KICC), Kenya Seed Company Limited, Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC), and New Kenya Co-operative Creameries.
How is the privatisation programme established in Kenya?
The privatisation initiatives in Kenya are guided by the 1998 Policy Paper on Public Enterprises Reform and Privatisation and the Privatisation Act 2005. These initiatives are part of the government's efforts for fiscal consolidation and economic development.
What are the benefits of privatisation in Kenya?
Privatisation benefits include improved regulation of the economy by reducing conflicts between the public sector's regulatory and commercial functions and increased efficiency by making the economy more responsive to market forces.
Who is the CEO of the Privatisation Commission of Kenya?
The CEO of the Privatisation Commission of Kenya is Dr. Joseph Koskey. He holds a PhD in Business Administration, an MBA in Strategic Management, and a Bachelor of Commerce in Accounting from the University of Nairobi.
What is the Privatisation Bill, 2023?
The Privatisation Bill, 2023, is a legislative framework aimed at regulating the privatisation of public entities, including state corporations. It establishes the Privatisation Authority and outlines the processes and guidelines for privatisation.
How does privatisation affect the Kenyan economy?
Privatisation can positively affect the Kenyan economy by enhancing the efficiency and competitiveness of formerly state-owned enterprises, attracting foreign investment, and reducing the fiscal burden on the government.
What role does the Privatisation Authority play?
The Privatisation Authority is responsible for formulating, managing, and implementing the privatisation programme. It also makes and implements specific proposals for privatisation in accordance with the programme.
What are the challenges associated with privatisation?
Challenges associated with privatisation include potential job losses, public opposition, regulatory challenges, and ensuring that privatised entities serve the public interest while maintaining profitability.
How can the public access information about ongoing privatisation initiatives?
The public can access information about ongoing privatisation initiatives through the official website of the Privatisation Authority, public notices, and reports published by the Authority.
